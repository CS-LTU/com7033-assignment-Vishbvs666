Learn about stroke

Simple information to help you understand stroke risk and prevention.

What is a stroke?

A stroke happens when the blood supply to part of the brain is blocked or reduced. Brain cells begin to die within minutes.

Quick treatment is critical. If you or someone near you has sudden weakness on one side, trouble speaking, or face drooping, call emergency services immediately.

Common risk factors

  • High blood pressure
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • High cholesterol
  • Obesity and lack of exercise

The StrokeCare model uses information like these factors to estimate your risk. Improving lifestyle and following your doctor's advice can help lower your chances of stroke.

Important disclaimer

StrokeCare is a demonstration project for learning data science and artificial intelligence. It is not a medical device and should not be used on real patient data. Always follow the guidance of licensed healthcare professionals.
